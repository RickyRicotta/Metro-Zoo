01 — Brand Strategy

"We are the city as ecosystem — feral, curious, alive with strange frequencies and unexpected beauty."

The Narrative

Metro Zoo operates at the crossroads of urban grit and cosmopolitan curiosity. The name itself is the thesis: a metropolis is a zoo — not in the diminishing sense, but in the sense of radical biodiversity, strange coexistence, and spectacular life lived in close quarters.

Where other DJ collectives chase scenes or scenes chase them, Metro Zoo builds habitats. Each set is an ecosystem: house pulses like a heartbeat, dub opens space like a forest clearing, jazz introduces dissonance and wonder, balearic dissolves the city into ocean air. The music moves like migration — purposeful, seasonal, alive.

Alvi, Ricky, and Baby are naturalists as much as selectors. They excavate records the way field biologists document specimens — with deep knowledge, genuine reverence, and a kind of joy that comes from finding something rare in the wild.

Strategic Position

Metro Zoo occupies white space between two poles: the hyper-specialized genre collector (too narrow) and the vibe-first DJ-as-brand (too shallow). Metro Zoo's competitive moat is breadth with depth — they can move from dub to jazz to balearic within a single night because each territory has been truly studied, not sampled.

This positions them uniquely for:

· Venues that value musical intelligence and crowd education over hype cycles
· Residencies that reward loyalty and scene-building
· Listeners who are tired of algorithmic taste and crave curation with a point of view
· Press and platforms that document culture rather than chase it

02 — Brand Archetype
THE
EXPLORER
+ SAGE

The primary archetype is The Explorer — driven by wanderlust, drawn to the undiscovered, allergic to formula. The secondary is The Sage — deep knowledge not worn as superiority but as generosity, sharing what's been found on the road.

This combination is rare in electronic music, where brands tend toward The Rebel (cool by opposition) or The Jester (cool by irreverence). Metro Zoo's duality gives them range: they can be adventurous and authoritative, warm and nerdy, accessible and deep.

Discovery — new sounds, new connections, new ways of hearing

Deep curation that opens ears to what they didn't know they needed

Formulaic sets, genre traps, forgettable nights

Obscurantism — avoid the trap of gatekeeping the very joy you're sharing

Reference World

National Geographic crossed with a dusty record shop. David Attenborough with a Technics 1200. The feeling of finding a $2 record that ruins you for a week. The moment a dance floor collectively realizes they're somewhere special. Naturalists with disco heads and jungle sensibilities.

Dry Wit

Accent register — used sparingly

When to use

Meme-format social posts, Reels/Stories humor, replies and comments, in-shop event copy, late-night social posting. Never in booking materials.

  • Spending $40 on a record you've never heard is called optimism.
  • If you know what this record is, please don't tell anyone at the bar.
  • Set reportedly described as "danceable but also kind of sad." We'll take it.
deadpan understated knowing self-aware never try-hard

Sound Like This

  • Specific about music — name records, artists, moments
  • Invite people into knowledge without gatekeeping
  • Let excitement show — it's infectious, not uncool
  • Write about how music feels in a body, in a room
  • Acknowledge the collective — Alvi, Ricky, and Baby as voices
  • Reference the city, the hour, the physical context of playing
  • Use plain language even about complex ideas

Never Sound Like This

  • Generic hype copy ("incredible vibes," "fire set")
  • Pretentious music criticism or name-dropping for status
  • Overly promotional — "the best DJ collective in NYC"
  • Detached or ironic in a way that creates distance
  • Aggressive self-marketing without substance
  • Trend-chasing language that dates in six months
  • Anything that makes the listener feel like an outsider
04 — Messaging Hierarchy
01
Brand Line
THE CITY IS A ZOO. WE ARE ITS DJS.

Primary brand statement. Not a tagline for every post — an internal north star. Informs all copy. Connects name to purpose, urban setting to sonic range.

Used on: RA header, booking decks, profile bios. The sentence that answers "who is Metro Zoo?"

02
Mission
Every record is a species. Every set is an expedition.

Translates the naturalist/explorer brand DNA into music language. Positions curation as genuine intellectual and emotional labor.

Used in: mix descriptions, long Instagram captions, bio sections on Soundcloud and Mixcloud. Conveys what they do and why it matters.

03
Collective
Three heads, one ecosystem.

Acknowledges the three-person structure. The "ecosystem" framing signals that differences in taste are a feature — they create diversity, not division.

Used in: booking inquiries, artist introductions, social content that features individual members. Celebrates multiplicity.

04
Capabilities
House. Dub. Jazz. Disco. Balearic. World. Digital or vinyl. Your room.

Functional message for booking contexts. Demonstrates range and flexibility without reducing to genre labels. "Your room" signals adaptability to venue needs.

Used in: RA event listings, booking emails, venue one-pagers. The message that gets you the gig.

05
Social Hook
We went to the shop. We went deep. Here's what happened.

Content series framing. The "In The Shop" format — referencing Razor-N-Tape's record shop — becomes a recurring content archetype. Discovery documented in real time.

Used in: Instagram Reels, Stories, record-shopping content. Maintains community connection between bookings. Drives organic reach via relatable crate-digging content.

08 — Resident Advisor Profile Template
Metro Zoo are Alvi, Ricky, and Baby — three selectors working out of New York City whose sets read like field dispatches from an ongoing expedition through recorded music. They move between house, dub, jazz, disco, balearic, and world music not as a stylistic exercise but as a genuine attempt to trace the underground routes that connect all of it. Having played Cafe Balearica, Razor-N-Tape, Lise & Vito, Jupiter Disco, and Sauced, they've built a reputation for sets that feel both deeply researched and utterly in the moment. They play digital or vinyl. They play until the room finds itself.

Event Description Template

Metro Zoo (Alvi, Ricky, Baby) return to [VENUE] for [a night of / an afternoon of / a long session in] [brief description of sonic territory — e.g. "searching, balearic-tinged house and disco" / "deep jazz-inflected selections and dub excursions"]. Expect the unexpected — they've never played the same set twice and they're not about to start.

  • Always list all three members by first name
  • Use sonic adjectives, not genre lists alone
  • One sentence on what makes this night specific
  • Keep tone warm — not promotional, not cold
  • Never use: "incredible," "fire," "unmissable," "vibes"

Tag & Metadata Strategy

  • Primary tags: House, Disco, Jazz, World Music, Balearic
  • Secondary: Dub, Eclectic, Record Collector
  • Never add Techno, EDM, or trend-adjacent tags for exposure
  • Event time: always list doors + music start separately
  • Ticket price: free when possible (builds community faster)
  • Event photo: bold animal poster, no busy collages
  • Link Soundcloud in every event: "Listen before you come"
  • Venue description: write it yourself, don't copy-paste

Engagement Strategy

  • Respond to all RA comments within 24hrs after events
  • Add upcoming events to RA calendar minimum 3 weeks ahead
  • Follow and track RA picks for similar NYC-region acts
  • Submit mixes to RA Podcast once established (Mix 010+)
  • Cross-link RA profile from Instagram bio and Soundcloud
  • Attend other listed RA events to build scene reciprocity

Booking Materials

  • One-pager PDF: logo, bio (50 words), genre list, venues, contacts
  • Tech rider: CDJ or Technics, standard mixer, separate monitor mix
  • Set length tiers: 2hr / 3hr / open-to-close — price accordingly
  • Venue type fit: listening bars, intimate clubs, loft parties, daytime
  • Preferred set times: peak (12–3am) or closing (3am–late)
  • Contact: single shared booking email, respond within 48hrs
09 — Visual Language System

The Animal System

Each animal in Metro Zoo's visual language is selected for its resonance with a sonic territory. Animals always appear in high-contrast duotone treatment — one flat color field, one accent. Never realistic photography without treatment.

  • Fox → Balearic. Clever, twilight-dwelling, borderless.
  • Toucan → House. Tropical, rhythmic, unexpectedly joyful.
  • Heron → Jazz. Patient, precise, sudden movement.
  • Sloth → Dub. Slow, heavy, all-consuming presence.
  • Frog → World Music. Amphibious, borderless, calls across distances.
  • Peacock → Disco. Extravagant, ecstatic, fully committed to color.
  • Pigeon → NYC / Local. Underestimated. Completely at home.
  • Eagle/Hawk → Open-to-close. Sees the whole terrain.

Poster System

  • Format: portrait, 4:5 ratio for feed, 9:16 for stories
  • Structure: Venue + date (top, Space Mono) / Animal (center) / METRO ZOO (large Bebas) / Artists (small Bebas) / Details (bottom, Space Mono)
  • Color: always one flat background + one accent, no gradients
  • Animal: always treated in duotone matching poster palette
  • Typography: all Bebas Neue for display, Space Mono for info
  • White space: generous — let the animal breathe
  • No drop shadows, no texture overlays, no grain effects
  • Always: ALVI · RICKY · BABY at bottom, same size

Photography Direction

  • In-shop: warm, natural light. Records visible. People in motion.
  • At-venue: low light, authentic — no flash photography on the night
  • Candid over posed — catch the room, not the pose
  • Edit palette: warm, slightly desaturated. Never HDR or heavily filtered.
  • Avoid: DJ booth hero shots. Show the crowd relationship.
  • Travel content: cities as ecosystems. Streets, markets, record shops.
  • Collective portraits: all three, active context, not a press shot.
